Understand the fundamentals of Electronic Data Interchange and why it's critical for modern supply chains.

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) allows businesses to exchange information electronically using standardized formats such as ANSI X12 and EDIFACT. It replaces paper-based transactions like purchase orders and invoices with automated, secure data flows.

Implementing EDI improves speed, accuracy, and cost-efficiency across the supply chain, making it a critical tool for industries like retail, logistics, and healthcare.
